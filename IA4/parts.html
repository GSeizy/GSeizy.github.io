PC Parts

Every PC is made up of several key parts, more commonly known as components.
Learning and understanding the function of each component will take you from beginner to pro.

The Essential Parts

The CPU (Central Processing Unit)

Quite literally, the CPU is the brain of your computer. It handles all the complex thinking and math. Depending on its clock speed, a typical CPU makes billions and trillions of calculations per second... Yes, you read that right.

The CPU Cooler

Your CPU generates a lot of heat when it's working hard, and without proper cooling it will overheat and shut down to protect itself. No CPU means no PC. There are two main types: First is air coolers (big heatsinks with fans) and liquid coolers (uses water to transfer heat away). Regardless of your choice, both work quite well. Air coolers are simpler and cheaper, while liquid coolers can be quieter and look cleaner. On the other hand, air coolers can be louder, and liquid coolers can fail... use your imagination there. Regardless take care of your PC and it will run well.

Fans

Sticking with the body analogy, fans are like lungs. They keep air flowing through the system. Intake fans inhale fresh, cool air for your PC to breathe and exhale hot air that is bad for it. Most cases come with one or two fans, but adding more can improve temperatures and give you more customization options.

The GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

This is the visual cortex of the PC. This takes all those CPU calculations and turns them into the conceivable information that is viewable to your eyes. Without the GPU, it's just 1's, 0's, and a blank screen. This part is essential for gaming and video editing.

RAM (Random Access Memory)

This is your PC's short term memory. The job of RAM is to temporarily store the information of whatever you're actively working on and then send it back to storage when you're done. It makes it so your computer runs faster and more efficiently. The more RAM you have, the more you can have open at once. If the main PC storage is the vault in a bank, the RAM is a safety deposit box. Essentially, it gets pulled when you need it, and it stays safely tucked away when you don't.

Storage

This is your PC's long term memory. It is where everything you store on your computer lives. Download an app? Goes to Storage. Upload and save an image? Goes to Storage. There are two types, SSD (Solid State Drive) and HDD (Hard Disk Drive). SSD is a solid, non-moving form of storage that is more reliable. HDD is kind of like a CD. It spins at thousands of RPMs and reads the storage electromagnetically. Both are slower than RAM and act as a long-term parking spot for anything on your PC. When you need it, RAM goes and grabs it for you to use smoothly, and then it goes back when you're done. Ever notice how the first time you open an application after a while, it can be slow to start? That's because the RAM has to go get it again.

The PSU (Power Supply Unit)

The beating heart of the PC. It does exactly what it says. It supplies power to the entire PC and without it, nothing runs. In most cases, it just looks like a small box with a cooling fan that vents out of the back of the PC. Next to that fan will be where your electricity supply (the wall power cord) will go. On one of the other sides are multiple leads that go to various parts of the pc to deliver power. I'd recommend choosing this wisely. The formula typically goes: more expensive part = more power needed. Not enough power means the PC won't run. Buy a cheap one that doesn't have good failsafes and the PC becomes prone to crashing, freezing, and or permanent damage.

The Motherboard

This part acts as the main connector for everything. How does your brain know that your foot is itchy? Because your nervous system runs all throughout your body and connects back up to your brain via the spinal cord (little anatomy lesson for you there.) The motherboard (spinal cord) holds every component together and tells the CPU (the brain) what they're doing every single nanosecond. The countless array of transistors and wires (nervous system) deliver the info to and from to accomplish this. Always make sure it's compatible with your CPU choice, RAM amount, and other connections you may prefer.

The Case

Finally, we have this case and this acts as the body. Just like how everything that makes you up is enclosed in your body; everything that makes up your PC is enclosed in its case. Be careful to pick one that fits your motherboard size, has good airflow, and room for the rest of your parts. Trust me on that, you don't want to get it all unboxed and assembled just to realize your GPU is too big to fit.

The Following Tools/Items:

  • Magnetic Phillips Screwdriver Set - Many screws in a PC are very small so a kit with #0, #1, and #2 phillips heads are recommended. A magnetic screw head is also incredibly useful and time saving as you will drop your screws countless times otherwise.
  • Anti-Static Wrist Strap - This helps to ground any electricity on your person. One small shock at the wrong time can fry components.
  • Zip Ties or Velcro Cord Straps - Crucial for good cable management. Trust me, you don't want cables everywhere!
  • Small Flashlight - Even in a well lit room, you're still working in a dark PC case. It gets hard to see.
  • Thermal Paste - Not always necessary as some CPU Coolers come pre-applied, but good to have if necessary.
  • USB Drive with Windows - All of your efforts will be for nothing if you don't have this. Can't use all those parts without an Operating System
