The History of Zelienople

Click one of the tabs below to discover Zelienople history!

The Man Who Started it All:

Baron Dettmar Basse was born in Germany in 1760 to a wealthy family. We have little information of his formative years, but we do know he was well educated and enjoyed politics as his career. Upon learning of the freedoms and liberties America (a very young country at the time) boasted, Dettmar knew he had to immigrate. Baron had many accomplishments during his time in Germany. Perhaps his most proud was his 600 acre estate in France. Though he sold this estate before his move to America. Arriving in Philidelphia in 1801, Basse was introduced to an opportunity to purchase 10,000 acres of land just outside of Pittsburgh. Using the money from his estate sale, Basse purchased the land that would become Zelienople. Not a bad trade if you ask us. The cards were set and Baron got to work incorporating this small settlement.

The Namesake:

Back home in Germany Baron's daughter, Frederika Wilhelmina “Zelie” Basse, was a young woman on the brink of marriage. Born in 1786, Zelie Basse had a legacy set before her that has stood the test of time. While her father was in America working to build the town that would become her namesake, Zelie was growing, learning, and falling in love. A prominent Frenchman named Philip Louis Passavant would eventually ask her hand in marriage, but there was one stipulation. Baron said he would only bless the marriage if the young couple came to live in America with him in Zelienople. Philip and Zelie married in Frankfurt, Germany in 1807 and then moved to America to begin their new life. As Zelienople grew through the years, so did the Passavant family. Growing to a family of Philip, Zelie, and their 5 children by 1821, the small borough only became more prosperous.

A Town is Born:

Before Zelie and her husband got to America, Baron kept very busy. In his new borough, he set to work building his 3 story castle, dubbed the Bassenheim. As he laid out the rest of the town, he incorporated a sawmill, iron furnace, and brickyard. Over the years, Zelienople grew from empty land to a small town. When Zelie and her husband arrived, Philip incorporated the town’s first mercantile right on main street. After this, he built what would become the town’s most important landmark, The Passavant House (pictured on the home page). Now owned by the Zelienople Historical Society, this is where Passavant and his family lived for generations until 1956.

A Modern Community With Old Time Flare:

Over two centuries later, Zelienople stands taller than it ever has before. Incorporating many modern amenities of the 21st century while still remembering our heritage. The town is now home to folks of all ages who walk the same streets the Dettmar and Passavant families once walked. Zelienople borough is packed full of community values, roaring good times, and a neighborly friendliness that would give even Mr. Rogers a run for his money. With everything from bustling restaurants to small boutique shops. Festivals year round to breathtaking sights and architecture. Feel free to stop on by today, we would love to have you!
